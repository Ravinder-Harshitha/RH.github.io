Tree Pose

Sanskrit name: Virshasana



How to do

This yoga pose involves tucking one leg into the other while your hands are in a prayer position above your head, resembling a tree. Bring your hands in front of your heart in Anjali Mudra (prayer position) or you can lift your arms up to the ceiling. Hands can remain shoulder-width apart. Stay in this pose for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Benefits of this pose

  1. Improves balance and stability in the legs.
  2. On a metaphysical level, helps one to achieve balance in other aspects of life.
  3. Strengthens the ligaments and tendon of the feet.
  4. Strengthens and tones the entire standing leg, up to the buttocks.
  5. Assists the body in establishing pelvic stability.
